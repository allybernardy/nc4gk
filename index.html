Our Mission

Where surplus finds its purpose

Every season, apparel brands are left with inventory that cannot move. Surplus runs, cosmetic irregulars, samples that never shipped. Putting it into the marketplace means competing with their own customers. Discarding it means waste. NC4GK offers a third way.

Across Southern California, millions of families live in genuine need. NC4GK exists at the intersection of these two realities, gathering what brands cannot use and delivering it, through verified Christian partners, to the families who need it most.

All product is given. No product is ever sold.The NC4GK Promise

Our Story

Where surplus finds its purpose

The Problem We Solve

Every season, brands are left with inventory they cannot move.

The apparel industry is built on margin, and surplus product threatens that margin the moment it hits the wrong channel. Overruns, cosmetic irregulars, off-spec runs, samples, end-of-season stock. Brands cannot put it into the marketplace without competing with their own customers. They cannot just throw it away. So it sits.

Southern California is home to a remarkable concentration of apparel and action sports companies. And it is also home to some of the deepest pockets of poverty in the country. NC4GK was founded on one simple insight: these two realities could solve each other.

The Need We Meet

Families across Southern California are waiting for what brands cannot sell.

Families in shelters. Children in underserved communities across Santa Ana, Anaheim, Lancaster, Hollywood, Van Nuys, Riverside, and South-Central LA. The need is consistent, deep, and close to home. In moments of disaster, we show up there too.

NC4GK coordinates with Salvation Army shelters, City Serve, and other verified Christian organizations to ensure every item gets to someone in genuine need. Not a discount bin, not a resale channel. Every item is a gift.

"Since 2014 the Salvation Army has distributed new clothes to people in need with NC4GK. We greatly value and appreciate our partnership, serving together to make a difference in the lives of others."
Kevin White, Past Communications Director, Salvation Army Orange County
How It Works

Simple process.
Real impact.

Over a decade refining a system that is effortless for brand partners and ensures every item reaches the right hands.

01

Brand donates inventory

Surplus, cosmetic irregulars, or off-spec runs collected from brand partners. We coordinate logistics so it is effortless on your end.

02

We intake and store

Every item is received, sorted, and held until matched to the right distribution event. This is where the real operational work happens.

Highest-cost step. Financial gifts help most here.
03

Distribution is coordinated

We work with verified Christian partners to plan distribution, matching inventory to communities and families in genuine need, careful not to create dependency.

04

Clothes reach families

Through trusted organizations, new clothing is placed directly in the hands of children and families, with a 100% no-resale guarantee at every step.*

* See the NC4GK Guarantee below.
Distribution Partners

Where the clothes go

FAQ

Common questions

What types of inventory do you accept?

We accept new, unworn clothing and apparel in any quantity. This includes surplus inventory, cosmetic irregulars, misprints, off-spec production runs, samples, and end-of-season overruns. We do not accept used, worn, or individually donated items.

What happens to the clothing after donation?

We intake and store the inventory at our Southern California facility, then coordinate distribution through verified Christian partners including the Salvation Army and City Serve. Every item is given directly to families in need. No product is ever sold.

Is my donation tax deductible?

Yes. New Clothes 4 God's Kids, Inc. is a registered 501(c)(3) non-profit corporation (FEIN: 47-2263205). Inventory and cash donations are tax deductible to the extent permitted by law. We can provide documentation upon request.

How do you guarantee clothing is not resold?

Every distribution partner is personally vetted and must hold a Christian mission with a verified commitment to direct distribution. We do not work with any organization that operates resale channels.

How long does the process take?

After submitting an inquiry we typically respond within one week. Coordination timelines vary based on inventory volume and location, but we handle logistics and keep you informed throughout.

Can we receive a donation receipt?

Yes. We provide documentation for all inventory donations. Please mention this when you submit your inquiry and we will make sure the right paperwork is included.

Do you accept small quantities or individual donations?

NC4GK is a B2B partner working with brands and manufacturers. If you have individual or gently used items to donate, your local Salvation Army or thrift store is the right fit.

How do I get started?

Fill out the inventory donation form and we will be in touch within one week. We will walk you through the process, coordinate logistics, and ensure your inventory reaches families in genuine need.
